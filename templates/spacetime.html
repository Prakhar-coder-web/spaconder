What is spacetime?

Earlier, Physicists used to think that space is just an empty thing/void. It's like a flat solid stage where events of this Universe are unfurling. The earlier thought process was that space is one different thing and time is another thing. Then in 1666, the apple fell on Sir Isaac newton leading to the discovery of gravity. After a lot of research and study, Newton published in his work that gravity is a force acting between two masses. He described gravity as the force which acts between two bodies having some mass.

But something was missing. The explanation which he gave on gravity was a little incomplete and unsatisfactory. He covered the aspects of this gravitational force but didn't know exactly what gravity is and how it propagates. He said there is a gravitational force between two masses, but that still doesn't explain why in the first place do two bodies having masses attract each other. Then in 1916, Albert Einstein published general relativity. It revolutionized the idea of gravity. It offered a better and accurate explanation for gravity and space.

"Spacetime tells matter how to move; matter tells spacetime how to curve"

In einstein's general relativity, he explained that space and time are not two different things but just one thing - spacetime. So instead of considering space and time as two separate entities, we have to treat them as a single entity called spacetime. His general relativity states that space and time are interwoven to form the mesh or fabric of spacetime which combines three dimensions of space and one dimension of time to form a 4-d spacetime. This spacetime is a fabric that gets distorted due to mass. It means that the spacetime bends around a massive object with huge mass or simply mass causes spacetime to curve around it.

In the picture above, the spacetime is getting curved around the Earth due to its mass. The more the mass or density of the Earth is, the more will be the curvature. Hence we can say that space is non-euclidean. But what do euclidean and non-euclidean mean? Euclidean geometry means in simple words, the regular geometry that tells the sum of all angles of triangles is 180 deg or two parallel lines never intersect. In non-euclidean geometry, the normal geometry rules are not followed, which means that two parallel lines can intersect and there can be a 5-sided square in non-euclidean geometry. The curvature of spacetime makes it non-euclidean.

The warping of spacetime creates the effect of gravitational force. This warping deflects the path of other objects. Like, imagine a trampoline. If you put a bowling pin at the center of the trampoline, the trampoline would curve around the object due to its mass. If we let a small marble roll on the trampoline, instead of going in the straight path, it will start revolving around the object as it's curving the trampoline around it. Light traveling in a straight line also gets curved or bent around heavenly planets due to the spacetime curvature. General relativity has been corroborated several times. Using telescopes like the Hubble telescope, it has been seen and observed that light bends around massive planets or stars. Other phenomena like Gravitational lensing also prove the same.
