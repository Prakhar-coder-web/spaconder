Why can’t an object with mass travel at the speed of light or greater than it?

You know that Einstein’s theory of relativity states that as the speed of the object approaches the speed of light, its mass increases. Now the concept of mass increasing is misleading so instead of mass increasing, visualize it as energy of the body increasing.

"It followed from the special theory of relativity that mass and energy are both but different manifestations of the same thing - a somewhat unfamiliar conception for the average mind. Furthermore, the equation E = mc², in which energy is put equal to mass, multiplied by the square of the velocity of light, showed that very small amounts of mass may be converted into a very large amount of energy and vice versa."

More the velocity of the body, more is the energy that means the faster a body moves through space, more the energy is required to accelerate the body. Now, once the speed of object reaches very close to the speed of light, almost infinite energy would be required to move or accelerate it and once it reaches the speed of light, infinite energy would be required to move or accelerate it which is impossible.

Just think you need to apply infinite energy to move the object. Once you apply infinite energy, the object will move or accelerate. But infinite means never ending so it would take never ending energy and thus infinite time to move and hence the object will not move or accelerate. On the other hand light consists of photons. Photons are massless so this doesnt apply on them

How nature restricts you to travel at a speed less than that of light?

Many people say that they have an idea of violating the fundamental physics law which states that the nothing can go faster than the speed of light.
Suppose There is a man inside a spaceship which is travelling in space. Suppose the spaceship accelerates to a speed slightly less than the speed of light. Now once, the spaceship reaches this speed, that man will start walking inside his spaceship. The idea is that the speed of spaceship + speed of man walking inside will exceed the speed of light.

Now suppose there is a woman standing outside the spaceship. From the woman’s perspective, time is running very slow in the spaceship and hence the speed of the man’s walking is also very very slow, hence the speed of the man and his spaceship will not exceed the speed of light. Now the more the speed of spaceship increases, more the time will flow slowly.

Once the speed of the spaceship reaches the speed of light, time will stop completely in the spaceship, so the walking of the man will also stop completely and hence the speed of the man will be zero and it will not exceed the speed of light.
