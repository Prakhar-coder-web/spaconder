Quantum computers

Quantum mechanics is one of the world's toughest fields of science and no one has been able to fully understand its underlying principles. One of its applications is Quantum computers. Quantum computers are not just different in design or size they are different at the fundamental level, that is the way they process the data. Quantum computers are 158 times faster than the worlds greatest supercomputers and they can perfom huge calculations so fast that they are also called the future of computing.

First Lets talk about how normal computers process data. Normal computers have processors. The smallest and the most fundamental unit of a processor is a transistor. Transistors are kind of switches that allow electrons(electricity) to flow through them. A transistor either allows electrons to flow through or the block/prevent the flow of electrons. You all know computers store data in the forms of bits which are in the forms of 0s and 1s. So all the data is stored in form of 0 and 1. In a transistor when current flows through it is represented by 1 and when it blocks the electrons, it is represented by 0. These transistors by allowing or blocking the electrons store data. More the number of transistors, the faster the processing speed and its efficiency.

Several transistors form logic gates which then combine with other logic gates to form basic modules, then chips. The current size of a transistor is 14nm. Less the size of the transistor, more transistors can be used and therefore more efficiency. There is a limit to which we can reduce the size of the transistor. Once we reduce the size of a transistor to the size of an atom then things start going weird because at this level quantum physics acts upon it. If we will use a transistor the size of the atom, then the electrons will simply transfer themselves to the other side of the transistors due to the property of quantum entanglement.

To solve this problem we can use quantum computers. Like in classical computers we use bits to store data, in quantum computers we use quantum bits which are Qubits. Instead of transistors, we use photons, a nucleus of an atom, or electrons to store data. Let's understand how this works. Let us take electrons as an example. Electrons have two possible quantum states, spin up or spin down. Now spin up represents 1 and the spin-down represents 0. But the weird part is here. In classical computers, a bit can take only one value either 0 or 1 but in quantum computers, the qubits can take either 1, 0, or even both. Yes!

The Qubits can be in a state of both the possible values which is 0 and 1 due to superposition(superposition is a phenomenon in which a particle is in two states at one like for eg an electron is an electron and is not an electron at the same time then we can say the electron is in superposition). Althought when we measure the Qubit it takes up the value of either 1 or 0. Turns out that the superposition is a blessing. In classical computers, 4 bits can represent one of 16 values possible, but in quantum mechanics, the Qubits can be all the 16 values possible at once.

Now through this example, we can easily understand why are quantum computers so fast. Suppose there is a maze and the cat is at the starting point of the maze and it wants to go to the endpoint of the maze to get food. It can only go through the maze to reach its destination. If classical computers are used to find the route through the maze they will check and try to check the route until the correct one is found(because bits can only represent one value at a time). If we use quantum computers the Qubits would represent all the possible routes at once and then it will simply collapse to give the right answer.

For example, if I tell you that have to find out two numbers(using hit and trial method) that would add up to 10 but their product is 16, so the bits in classical computers would try each value until it finds the correct one while the Qubits in a quantum computer would represent all possible combinations of numbers that would add up to 10 and then it would simply collapse to the correct answer.
